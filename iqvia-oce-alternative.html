Alternative guide

Bring-your-own-data HCP targeting, without the OCE engagement

IQVIA's OCE suite and OneKey reference data power omnichannel engagement for large commercial organizations. They're bought through enterprise engagements — data licensing, implementation, managed services. If your need is narrower — you already have HCP lists and you need to score, segment, and export them defensibly — LiteHCP Targeter covers that workflow with a single self-hosted PHP file.

Two different philosophies

IQVIA model: data + platform + services

  • Verified HCP universe (OneKey) licensed from the vendor
  • Omnichannel orchestration inside the suite
  • Managed implementation and support
  • Data flows through vendor infrastructure

LiteHCP model: your data, your server

  • You supply the lists (conferences, partners, data vendors, internal systems)
  • Scoring, rules, and segments computed locally in SQLite
  • Deploy yourself in minutes on any PHP 8 host
  • Nothing leaves your infrastructure; exports are privacy-screened CSVs

What you can do on day one

  • Import a messy CSV and impute gaps explicitly (fixed / mode / mean / flag-null) — every imputed value stays visibly marked.
  • Score every profile for priority (consent, recency, engagement) and data confidence (0–100).
  • Run JSON rules that assign personas and compliance flags — including a suppression rule for no-consent HCPs.
  • Build segments with safe SQL-like filters and a strict mode that demands minimum data quality.
  • Simulate campaign cost and ROI per segment before committing budget.
  • Export minimal-field CSVs; PII export is admin-gated and audited.
no procurement cycle — clone the repo or download one file, upload it to hosting you already have, and click Load sample to explore a realistic 200-profile dataset immediately.

Where IQVIA remains the right call

If you need a continuously-verified national HCP universe, regulated omnichannel execution at scale, or contractual SLAs and managed services, an enterprise engagement is what actually delivers that. LiteHCP is deliberately small: it's the targeting bench tool, not the commercial data estate.
